Faraday's Law states that a time varying magnetic field accompanies a spatially varying, non-conservative electric field:

$$
\nabla \times \vec{E} = -\frac{\partial \vec{B}}{\partial t}
$$

Using [[Stokes Theorem]] this can be written in integral form:

$$
\oint_{\partial S} \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l} = - \int_S \frac{\partial \vec{B}}{\partial t} \cdot d\vec{A}
$$

and for a fixed surface the RHS simplifies to the time derivative of the flux,

$$
\oint_{\partial S} \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l} = - \frac{d\Phi_B}{dt}
$$
